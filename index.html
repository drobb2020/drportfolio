My First Career

Marine Engineer with the Canadian Coast Guard

In 1979 after graduating from high school I applied for and was accepted into the Marine Engineering program at the Canadian Coast College, located in Sydney, Nova Scotia, Canada. The three year program trained me in engine operation and maintenance, a full range of machine shop operations including working with lathes through welding. The academic courses included ship construction and architecture, along with electrical theory, and of course diesel engine theory and design. The cirriculum was heavy in math, physics and chemistry as well. The summer periods between each year were spent working in the machine shop, or on sea duty in the fleet. At the end of the program I took and passed the fourth Class combined Marine Engineering certificate. Upon graduation I was assigned to the CCGS Camsell on the west coast of Canada, sailing out of Victoria BC. I served on the Camsell initially as third engineer, and was promoted to second engineer, and was acting as first engineer when I left the ship. During this time I spent 3 1/2 summers in the Western Canadian Arctic with the CCGS Camsell. I was reassigned to the construction team on the CCGS George R. Pearkes in early 1986, and helped commission the ship and was first engineer for the first year of the shipâ€™s operation. In 1987 I transferred to the East Coast and worked on multiple ships as Senior Engineer and a couple of ships as Chief Engineer. In 1988 I transferred to the Great Lakes and worked initially in the Regional Office and later on all the ships in the region. Eventually I was promoted to Chief Engineer of the CCGS Simcoe. I finally retired from the sea in 1994. It was a great first career! David Robb
